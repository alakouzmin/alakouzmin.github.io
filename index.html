• Yuri Kuzmin is a sound artist and instrument builder based in Hangzhou and, occasionally, Hong Kong. He is interested in tools, media, mending and maintenance.
  • 2010-2016: BA Sinology, HSE, Moscow, Russia

    2016-2019: MFA Media Arts, School of Intermedia Art, China Academy of Art, Hangzhou, China

    2021-now: PhD student, Computational Media and Arts, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology.

  • Projects


    Some of the projects undertaken over the years.
  • finger.print. 2020
  • An attempt to make a book for a show about writing. Solderpads of a loop playback circuit are exposed as a text snippet encoded in the braille characters. Solar panels powering the machibe were salvaged from the destroyed bicycles left in the wake of the sudden collapse of several major bicycle sharing platforms in China. Sounds and text were provided by Yerevan-based poet Oleg Belavskiy.

    I remember there being something about (hermeneutic) circles but that bit of reasoning somehow escapes my memory.
  • wave spindle. 2021.
  • A primitive patchable video synthesizer weaving tapestries of mismatched framerates and interference patterns.

    The resulting patterns are dependant on the framerate on the phone camera.
  • water writing. 2018.
  • A noisy synthesizer, a collection of open circuits on a flat surface connected with water droplets. A chinese ink brush was used to this end as the most sophisticated water dispenser out there. Mooncake box enclosure was handpicked from the aftermath of the Mid-Autumn festivities.
  • 竹脑(zhu nao). 2023.
  • A ML powered instrument/interface for environmental improvisation inspired by the traditional materials and geometries of Chinese carpentry. Initially conceived as an exploration of found objects and shapes, this project gradually shifted focus towards the idea of found environments (pockets of WLAN coverage, patches of exposed ground) while retaining the improviser’s agency to express themselves sonically with and within the said environments.

    With code contributions from Clarence Cheung and Kenny Ma.
  • electric hurdy gurdy. 2020.
  • An autonomously (hand-cranked) powered drone box insipred by hurdy-gurdy (and that one video of Keiji Haino playing one), barrel organ and this particular medieval illustration.
